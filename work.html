Graphic Design, Web Design, Production
 
WNYU
As Technology Director at WNYU I worked with the design firm Awesome Possum to herald in the ground up redesign of WNYU's web services. In addition to moving the station forward, I maintained and administrated the disparate network of infrastructure, a frankenstein monster that was slowly iterated upon over the greater part of a decade.
Graphic Design
A large part of the redesign required the generation of new art assets to promote and extend the brands of the shows on the station. This involved working with the DJs themself along with the DJ's content to develop individual identities based on original material ranging from small snippets of personality during ad breaks, to more involved podcasts personalities coming through podcasts.
Production
During my time at WNYU I held down a weekly 2-hour radio show called New New York 3k. This show highlighted niche electronic music with a focus on exploring internet scenes and liminal music genres such as Muzak and more avant stuff. The show ended with a station-first 24-hour long radio stream which I promoted through print and trailer.
Guillo: The Smart Guillotine
As the capstone project for Integrated Digital Media I developed Guillo, a fictional tech startup hoping to make waves by creating "solutions" and "answers". The product itself was a piece o' junk pile of wood in the shape of a guillotine with a smart phone attached to it. The only victim of this guillotine? subtlety
Graphic Design
Part of recreating a modern tech company involved coming up with the appropriate marketing and display materials. This included graphs and posters to be able to try and deliver the spirit of the product quickly.
Web Design
Of course, no tech thing would be complete without their own very professional Bootstrap website. Including all of the hyped standards of the time guillo.website presents all of the rhythms and language that would make it seem like a good thing, even though it is, at best, mediocre.
Production
The whole undertaking required making a sort of "Brand Bible" for a fictional brand. The end result was a style of speaking, and a clear image that was trainable and easily reproducible. Me and drafted other students at the showcase were able to make startlingly similar demos based on the strength of the core messaging of this product.
Livestream Livecode
Created a performance based on Live Coding Youtube by Sang Won Lee, Jungho Bang, Georg Essl.
Production
Based on Live Coding Youtube I adapted the piece. The performance loads up a combination of youtube videos and twitch.tv livestreams, then plays them over each other becoming a improvisational noise piece about whatever's going on at that moment online. Then I begin to stream out the performance to twitch.tv, then insert my own stream into the performance, investigating redundancy and analog/digital noise of the endeavour.
